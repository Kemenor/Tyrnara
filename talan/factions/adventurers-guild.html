Origin

Founded during the Dark Era not by kings or gods but by conscience. Wanderers and soldiers with no banner began resisting the Corrupted God because their conscience demanded it. That founding history shapes everything about the Guild. It exists because mortals needed it to exist, and it answers to that mission before any government.

✦

Neutrality — how it actually works

The Guild's defining principle is strict neutrality within the law of whatever kingdom it operates in. If slavery is legal in a kingdom, the Guild will not post quests to free slaves — no law was broken. If a stolen object needs recovering, the Guild will post the quest; if the adventurers break the law in the process, the Guild will not publicly side with them — but they will not mind either. Individual members may work to overthrow unjust governments, free slaves, or undermine corrupt power. Never in official Guild capacity. This strict legalism is principled, not cowardly — it is what allows the Guild to hold relations with every kingdom simultaneously.

✦

Scale by location

Remote villages — A single person working part-time, sometimes managing four villages from one location. Towns — Real branch offices with proper staff, quest boards, contracts. Cities — Guild halls. Significant infrastructure, multiple staff, storage, holding cells. Capitals and city-states — Guild Complexes. The full weight of the institution: archives, legal staff, senior officers, direct contact with Guild leadership.
