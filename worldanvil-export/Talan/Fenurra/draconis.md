Draconis – The Scaled Flame

“To temper steel, you need fire. To temper a people, you need something far hotter.”
— Vaethryn Aex, Speaker of the Scar

Known as: The Scaled Flame, The Starborn, Forgemasters of Fenurra

Core Animal Influence: Ember Drake – mythic beasts said to have emerged from the meteor’s core, all winged fire and timeless vigilance

⸻

The Scar of Aeris

The Draconis are anchored in Aeris, the capital city of Fenurra, carved into the depths of the Scar of Aeris, an ancient meteor crater where star-metal met sulfurous soil and rewrote the rules of survival. Here, the earth bleeds heat and magic; the air hums with latent energy.

This crater is not just a place of power—it is the birthplace of Fenurran secrets. From it came:
	•	Blackglass, the legendary alloy of steel and obsidian
	•	Spice Wine, the hallucinogenic elixir of communion and clarity

The Draconis tribe are the keepers of both—guardians of craft, memory, and the subtle line between unity and annihilation.

⸻

Cultural Identity: Voice of the Flame

To be Draconis is to bear the mark of the Scar itself. Many are born with silver scales, embers in their hair, or smoldering eyes—gifts of slow exposure to the crater’s magic. They are not the largest or most numerous tribe, but they are the most refined, shaped by generations of proximity to elemental truth.

Draconis culture is measured, ceremonial, and flame-tempered. Their warriors are drilled to precision. Their diplomats choose words like others choose blades. But their control does not mean coldness—Draconis burns beneath the surface, and when they celebrate, it is with the full heat of life.

“We forge steel with fire—but we drink with joy.”
— Draconis toast before battle

They host Spice Wine ceremonies under the open sky, especially when diplomacy or alliance is needed. To sip with a Draconis is to share vision, shed lies, and dissolve grudges in fire-born truth. The wine, brewed only from sulfur-rich roots found near the Scar, is stored in bottles of crystal and obsidian, and is said to help one see through another’s eyes.

⸻

Blackglass & the Forging Flame

The Draconis are the exclusive forgers of Blackglass, an alloy made by lacing steel with obsidian mined from within the Scar. It is:
	•	Razor-sharp beyond compare
	•	Capable of cutting through solid metal
	•	Unforgiving—if struck incorrectly, it shatters like brittle glass

This duality has shaped their worldview: Power is only as good as the precision with which it is used. Every warrior learns the saying:

“Blades of Blackglass are sharper than the truth and swift as vengeance, but fragile if wielded without respect. A blade chooses its bearer and breaks in the hands of the unworthy.”

Only those who demonstrate restraint and discipline are permitted to wield it.

Their most renowned weapon-craft is the Drakar Talons:
Curved climbing claws forged from pure Velthite (star-metal alloy), designed for vertical assault, wall-scaling, and deadly close combat. These are more than weapons—they are rites of passage, and every Talon is sung into form by a forgesmith in a ritual known as the Breath of Shaping.

⸻

The Speaker’s Mantle

Draconis has held the Speaker’s Mantle for generations—not because they conquered, but because they proved themselves most capable of uniting those who cannot be ruled.

The Speaker is not a king or queen, but a voice strong enough to shape silence into consensus. Their duties include:
	•	Mediating tribal disputes
	•	Proposing unified actions (war, migration, diplomacy)
	•	Negotiating with outsiders without losing Fenurran dignity

It is said the best Speaker is not the loudest, but the one whose silence others lean toward.

The Speaker lives at the center of Aeris, beneath the Hall of Shards, surrounded by broken blades, shattered shields, and carved oaths—reminders that unity is never born from peace, but from forged tension.

⸻

Combat Role
	•	Velthite Guard: Elite spear and glaive formations armored in fire-tempered metal
	•	Talon-Climbers: Wall-scaling specialists equipped with Drakar Talons
	•	Command Strikers: Mid-line battlefield captains trained in elemental tactics
	•	Spice-Wine Seers: Combat-aware diplomats who fight and mediate when needed

Draconis never opens battle with a scream. They begin with measured movement, ritual pace, and a single warhorn note—echoed through the Scar like a god drawing breath.

⸻

Beliefs
	•	Fire is not destruction. It is refinement.
	•	The tongue can kill faster than the blade, but both must be sharpened.
	•	To sip Spice Wine is to see another’s truth. To wield Blackglass is to cut one’s own lies first.
	•	The Speaker holds not power, but balance—and balance burns hotter than chaos.

⸻

Symbol

A silver-scaled wing wrapped around a rising flame, or a draconic eye nested in shards of Blackglass—a symbol of vigilance, transformation, and the constant shaping of unity through fire.
