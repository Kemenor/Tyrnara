The War Council – Command by Firelight, Not Chain
“If you want followers, bring a better plan. If you want obedience, find another people.”
— Brakkaun proverb
In Fenurra, war is not commanded by rank—it is led by trust, consensus, and competence. There are no generals. No permanent hierarchy. The battlefield is not a chessboard. It is a crucible—and only those who can survive it, shape it.
This philosophy culminates in the institution of the War Council, the Fenurran system of military planning and action.

How It Begins: The Plan
A War Council begins when one Fenurran—warrior, scout, or shaman—has a plan for action. This individual becomes the War Speaker.
But a plan alone means nothing in Fenurra. Before calling the council, the speaker must first gather direct support from other warriors whose skills are essential to the execution.
For example, if a warrior proposes an Infernal-Smoked Hook-Line-Sinker siege, they must first gain backing from:
	• Drakar Talon climbers
	• Brakkaun shamans to craft infernal illusions
	• Vexiren archers to provide cover and smoke-eyed sight
Without this coalition, the plan has no spine—and the council will not follow.

Calling the Council
Once support is secured, the War Council is called.
It is an open gathering—any squad, pair, or bonded warrior may attend. During the council:
	• The War Speaker presents the plan
	• Any member can question, challenge, or offer alternatives
	• New tactics may be suggested
	• Squads and tribes may join or abstain freely
Fenurrans do not follow orders. They join causes. If a plan is flawed, it will collapse under scrutiny. If it is sound, it will grow stronger with every voice.

Variant Councils
War Councils may take different forms:
	• Initiative Councils: A full plan is proposed with partial support. The speaker must convince others to join and refine it.
	• Open Flame Councils: No plan exists—only a shared goal (e.g., reclaiming a fallen village). Strategy is forged collaboratively.
	• Ash Seat Trials: When no consensus emerges, or if confidence in the speaker fails, the council may challenge the War Speaker. The most competent tactician—by wit, record, or demonstrated insight—may take the mantle instead.
No blood is spilled in this process. The one who steps down is not shamed—they are respected for yielding to the better fire.

Philosophy of Fenurran Warfare
	• Command is earned. Not given.
	• Plans must breathe. Rigid chains break.
	• Those who act together survive together.
	• The best idea leads. Not the oldest. Not the loudest.

A war council may burn for hours by torchlight, every voice heard before blades are drawn. But when the council ends, and the rings are sealed, there is no doubt—only fire, trust, and motion.
