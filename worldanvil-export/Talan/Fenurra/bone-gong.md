The Bone Gong – Call of the Council

“No blade draws before the bone sings.”
— Fenurran axiom

The Bone Gong is the signal by which all tribes are called to speak as one. Carved from the femur of a Direbeast or great sulfur-stag and strung with meteor chain, it resonates with a deep, bone-shaking hum that carries through ash, stone, and silence alike. It is struck only by those who dare summon the minds of war.

Each number of strikes carries a meaning understood across all Fenurra:

    One Strike – Open Flame:
    A call to gather. No plan has yet been formed—only the will to face something together.

    Two Strikes – Initiative:
    A plan has been proposed, with partial support. Those hearing it may come to debate, refine, or join.

    Three Strikes – War Council:
    A formal strategy has gathered its pillars. The tribes are called to align behind action.

    Five Strikes – Emergency:
    No discussion. No delay. This is the sound that wakes a camp at night and sends it to the walls.

Additionally, during an ongoing War Council, a single strike is understood by all as the invocation of an Ash Seat Trial—a challenge to the current War Speaker’s position. It is not a threat, but a rite: when a better mind steps forward, the fire must shift to hold it.

No tribe owns the Bone Gong. It is kept near every tribal hall, sometimes duplicated in smaller forms. But the largest and oldest still hangs in the Scar of Aeris, its chain blackened with the soot of a thousand choices.
