Draconic Double-Glaive
“She didn’t charge. She wove through the line and left pieces behind.”

This is the signature mounted weapon of Draconis glaivemasters—a double-headed polearm forged to harness the full movement capabilities of Fenurran Direwolves. Unlike horses, Direwolves are agile in all directions: they sidestep, backpedal, leap, and pivot with predatory grace. The Double-Glaive was designed to match that precision.

It is not wielded with sweeping flair but with brutal efficiency, flowing in tandem with the rider's mount to strike, withdraw, and reposition in a single fluid motion. It is not a lance for charging, but a blade for sliding between weaknesses, delivering rapid, lethal cuts as the rider dances through chaos.

Purpose:
Close and mid-range mounted combat weapon for agile strike-and-withdraw tactics

Construction:
• Shaft forged from craterwood core wrapped in ironlace—flexible but durable under torque
• Twin blades affixed at both ends, forged from refined steel and blackstone edge material
• Blades are single-edged, slightly curved, designed for slicing on the move, not heavy cleaving
• Weight-balanced for spinning techniques, reactive strikes, and rapid direction changes

Function:
• Used from Direwolf-back in weaving combat—slash left while sidestepping right, cut high then spin low
• Allows the rider to strike in any direction, without needing to turn their mount
• Ends can be reversed mid-motion, or the shaft choked up for closer work
• Disarms, disables, and kills in a fluid series of calculated moves, never wasted energy

Wielded by:
Draconis riders trained in motion-based combat
Rare outside the tribe due to the coordination and balance required between weapon and mount
Often carried alongside a dagger or Talon for finishing if dismounted

Notes:
Most wielders etch a single line of text into the haft—not as a motto, but as a reminder to themselves of why they must keep moving. A survival truth. A loss they won’t repeat.
