Claw Dart & Bastion Ringblade

“My reach is yours. Your guard is mine. Together, we are the horizon, and it is closing.”
— Bond-duel vow of the Seravain elite

When a Seravain pair rises above mirrored tactics, they are entrusted with an elite arsenal forged not for compatibility—but for interdependence. The Claw Dart and Bastion Ringblade are two weapons, two roles, one soul. Their wielders move in a shared rhythm, striking across the battlefield in unison, extending each other’s reach and crushing any enemy who dares separate them.

Claw Dart – Rope Dart Variant
Purpose: Rapid precision strikes, arcing threat projection, weapon coordination
Construction:
• Bladed head resembling a hooked talon or fanged claw
• Tethered by braided embercord, resistant to heat and tension strain
• Weighted for return arcs, drag strikes, and snap-pierces
• Mounted loop-ring allows temporary bracing on partner’s gauntlet, weapon, or even armor

Function:
• Arcs through enemy lines or over cover to harass, bind, or disrupt
• Can embed into shields, torsos, or limbs, then retracted or redirected
• Used to pull the Ringblade back mid-flight, slingshot it forward, or even anchor it in circular sweeps
• Mastered users can entangle, disable, and strike without breaking stride

Wielded by:
The more mobile or reactive partner, often the one who leads movement and creates chaos.
Personal claw darts often bear etched flame-knot runes—signatures of the bond’s uniqueness.

Bastion Ringblade – Reinforced Circular Blade
Purpose: Sweeping cleaves, defensive parries, ranged throw-pull tactics
Construction:
• Massive circular blade with an inner grip and reinforced outer edge
• Balanced for both mid-range melee and ranged throws
• Built with friction grooves to catch and redirect the Claw Dart’s tether
• Core frame forged from cratersteel laced with bone inlay for shock absorption

Function:
• In melee: blocks, sweeps, trips, and overwhelms groups with circular motion
• Thrown: spins through tight arcs or ricochets in confined spaces
• Reclaimed mid-air via the Claw Dart, or caught in synchronized pass
• Can be braced as a rotating guard while the partner repositions

Wielded by:
The anchor partner—less mobile, more defensive, built for force redirection and setting up devastating lines of attack
Some carve the inside ring with quotes from their bonding vows. Others embed shards of their Sulfur Fire wristbands into the frame.

Together:
The Claw Dart and Ringblade form a living system of offense, redirection, and return. Where one arcs, the other crashes. Where one is blocked, the other completes the blow. In battle, Seravain elite pairs move like a closing storm front, silent, precise, inescapable.
