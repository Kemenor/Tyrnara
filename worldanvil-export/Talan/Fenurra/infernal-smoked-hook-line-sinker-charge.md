# Infernal-Smoked Hook-Line-Sinker charge

**Type:** Article  
**Category:** Fenurra  
**Status:** WIP  
**Updated:** 2025-07-06

---

The Fenurran Infernal-Smoked Hook-Line-Sinker Charge—the answer to the high-walled arrogance of outsiders.

A masterpiece of ruthless strategy and psychological warfare, this assault doctrine was forged in the smoke-choked crucible of the Sulfur Wastes. The Infernal phase begins the attack: warriors and Direwolves are anointed with shamanic alchemy that sets their eyes ablaze and makes their hair flicker like open flame. Bone flutes, tied to the throats of charging Direwolves, catch the wind and release a symphony of wails—agonizing, unearthly screams that echo like the tormented damned. Against zealots and faithful, this illusion of demonic fury shatters morale before blade or fang ever draws blood.

Then comes the Smoked phase. Ashstones—alchemical devices of volcanic char and sulfur—are cracked open, releasing thick plumes of smoke that roll over the battlefield. While defenders are blinded and choking, Fenurran archers, adapted to the sulfur and gifted with heat-sight, pick off sentries along the ramparts with ruthless precision, every arrow a silent punctuation in the fog of war.

And finally, the Hook-Line-Sinker strikes like a serpent. Direwolves trained to run the vertical stone charge the walls, leaping and scaling with claw and fang. Riders use their Drakar Talons to climb higher still—the Hook. From above, they drop ropes down into the chaos—the Line. And then come the Sinkers: the Breakers, massive Direwolves bred for power, who ascend using the ropes to bring devastation to the battlements. When they crest the walls, the enemy’s heart sinks. Moments later, the gate swings open from within—and the Fenurran horde rides through the smoke like death itself.

To outsiders, it is sorcery. To Fenurrans, it is survival made perfect.
