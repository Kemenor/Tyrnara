Brakkaun Siege Splitter
“The first cut is clean. The second takes the wall with it.”

This weapon system was developed by Brakkaun close-combat specialists for siege interiors, urban brawls, and fortress corridors where meteor hammers are unwieldy and space is limited. It combines a bladed shield and a short sword, each functional on its own, but built to lock together into a devastating greataxe configuration for overwhelming, brutal swings.

It’s not elegant. It doesn’t need to be. The split-second transformation from shield-and-blade to axe-form is baked into Brakkaun combat patterns—part of the rhythm of killing, not a flourish.

Purpose:
Multi-form melee weapon designed for enclosed spaces and flexible offensive pressure

Construction:
• Triangular bladed shield: reinforced slagsteel face with sharpened lower edge and spike catch
• Short broad sword: heavy spine, wide belly for crushing weight; grip compatible with shield locking mechanism
• Locking mechanism uses press-latch and twist-channel—mechanical, rugged, repairable in the field
• Combined form becomes a broad-bearded greataxe, balanced at the join for centerline strikes

Function:
• In shield-and-sword form: block, push, slash, stab
• In greataxe form: break shields, sunder doors, cleave lines
• Seamless switch between forms during combat—attack-feint-snap-lock-swing-repeat
• Meant for fortress interiors, breach holds, and group pressure fights

Wielded by:
Brakkaun siege-breakers and inner-line assault teams
Used in three-stage tactics: block, bait, break

Notes:
Each shield-face is personalized with engravings, slashes, or embedded scraps of metal from previous sieges—not decorative, but reminders of every barrier they've overcome

Some Splitters carry scoring lines showing how many walls they've broken through—not for pride, but memory. A Brakkaun doesn’t count kills. They count doors opened.
