Fenurran Meteor Hammers
	“No blade. No flourish. Just mass, movement, and the sound of something breaking.”
	— Brakkaun breaker during plains conflict
Fenurran meteor hammers are sling-chain weapons built purely for open-field engagement, ideal for use at high speed or from Direwolf-back. They combine kinetic reach, heavy blunt trauma, and tribal adaptation to the demands of a brutal, wide-open battlefield.

1. Ashbinder (Elite Anti-Armor Variant)
	“Let their defense become their doom.”
Purpose: Elite weapon built to counter heavy Outsider armor
Construction:
	• Twin hammerheads forged from slagsteel, embedded with emberstones that ignite on motion
	• Spiked heads embed into plate, transferring heat inward
	• Connected by meteor metal chain—non-conductive, durable under extreme stress
Function:
	• Strike embeds the weapon
	• Emberstones superheat armor, causing burns, heatstroke, or internal roasting
	• Ideal against shield walls, fortress infantry, or arcane juggernauts
Wielded by: Elite breacher squads and veteran heavy strikers
Not used in tribal conflict—no Fenurran wears armor thick enough to justify it
Often the ember heads have messages carved into them.

2. Standard Meteor Hammer (Open Field Combat)
	“You don’t need fire if the first hit crushes his ribs.”
Purpose: Blunt-force weapon for open terrain brawling, cavalry engagements, and infantry surges
Construction:
	• Dual heads of scar-forged iron, sometimes capped with bone or slag flanges
	• Chain length optimized for range and arc speed
	• Often paired with wrist or waist retention cords for mounted use
Function:
	• Spun wide and loose on the run or gallop
	• Used to stagger formations, smash limbs, or rip through lines
	• Simple, brutal, dependable
Wielded by: Brakkaun shock infantry, mounted strikers, and forward skirmishers
Requires space to build momentum—not viable in tight quarters or confined terrain
Often wrapped in leather with personal carvings or worn trinkets.

3. Hooked Meteor Hammer (Direwolf Rider Variant)
	“It doesn’t snag—it tears.”
Purpose: Variant designed for mounted use, focused on disruption and directional force
Construction:
	• One head replaced by triple-bladed hook fang, forged from steel or craterbone
	• Other side remains a heavy, solid hammerhead
	• Balanced for momentum-driven use from a Direwolf’s back
Function:
	• Hooked into shields, limbs, or mounts
	• Rider uses the Direwolf’s movement and strength to rip through enemy lines, unseat riders, or cause pileups
Wielded by: Fast-moving wolf-riders, flank units, and mid-charge attackers
Often used to drag enemies from cover or rip apart barricades
