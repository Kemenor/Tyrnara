Common Fenurran Arrow types:
Obsidian Tipped:
Type: Balanced Ghost willow shaft with obsidian tips

Design: 
  •	Razor-sharp obsidian heads, brittle but catastrophic
  •	Upon impact, the tip burrows deep, and then shatters inside the wound
  •	Removing the shaft does not stop the bleeding—in fact, it ensures further damage
  •	Fenurran field medics describe the effect as “a wound that breathes until death”
Purpose: Lethality over everything else, if the target is not killed by the first hit, the bleeding will take them. Lacks Heavy armor penetration.

Spinehook
Type: Narrow hooked steel arrow
Design:
  •	Needle-sharp tip with slight lateral barb hook
  •	Designed to dig through mail and plates
  •	Once embedded, it causes muscle pull and pain movement, disabling limbs
Purpose:
Tactical and lethal, heavy armor breaker, but not as lethal as the obsidian tips, used to disable shield arms, legs, or slow down runners

Emberframe arrows:
    “Pain is a message. We just sharpened the handwriting.”
    — Fenurran Fletcher’s Proverb

 Base Type: Emberframe Shaft

The Emberframe is a specialized, hollow-core arrow used across Fenurra for payload delivery—chemical, explosive, or particulate. Unlike traditional Ghost Willow shafts, Emberframes are:
  •	Constructed from reinforced ashwood, craterbone, or bark-laced composites
  •	Heavier than standard arrows, designed for short-to-mid-range impact delivery
  •	Fitted with sealed impact tips—resin bulbs, brittle glass, or clay-wrapped packs that rupture on contact

Fenurrans use Emberframes for field control, psychological warfare, and strategic debilitation, not flashy kills. These arrows are considered tools of terrain and tempo, not heroics.

Emberframe Arrow Variants

1. Spicegas Arrow
	“Their gods didn’t save them from shadows they imagined themselves.”
Payload: Distilled Spice Wine waste mixed with spores from the Glimmerspore Shroom, a fungus found near Spice fermentation pools
Effect: On impact, releases a fast-acting hallucinogenic cloud that:
  •	Causes severe disorientation
  •	Triggers sensory illusions, often blending friend and foe
  •	Disrupts cohesion in tight formations or caster circles
Tactical Use:
  •	Perfect for ambushes, delaying reinforcements, or breaking shield lines before a charge
  •	Most effective when paired with smoke-heavy tactics—victims can’t tell if what they hear is real

2. Rotbreach Arrow
	“Let's see if we can drown their prayers in their screams.”
Payload: Tip coated with an ultra-persistent neurotoxin derived from the Dreyghe Thorn—a barbed creeping plant known for its near-mythic sting (akin to the Gympie-Gympie)
Effect:
  •	Causes intense, burning nerve pain that can last weeks or months
  •	Victims often hallucinate, scream, or attempt self-harm
  •	Does not kill—ensures suffering is heard, not silenced
Tactical Use:
  •	Used to break enemy morale; a single Rotbreach victim can demoralize an entire unit
  •	Especially effective when fired into ranks of fresh recruits or high-ranking officers
  •	Fenurrans often leave Rotbreach victims alive deliberately

3. Ashburst Arrow
	“They turned to find us. Then they found nothing at all.”
Payload: Mixture of volcanic salt, charroot powder, and a spark-seed core
Effect:
  •	Upon impact, triggers a flash-burst of smoke and cinders
  •	Releases a piercing sonic pop designed to disrupt hearing and concentration
  •	Within 5–10 feet, most targets experience temporary deafness, startle reflex, and visual obscuration
Tactical Use:
  •	Ideal for breaking formations, covering retreats, or launching surprise flanks
  •	Used frequently during Infernal-Smoked assaults and Hook-Line-Sinker wall runs

4. Shardsong Arrow
	“If the first didn’t kill him, the music did.”
Payload: Tightly packed bone needles, glass slivers, and resin bindings
Effect:
  •	On impact, the tip explodes outward, scattering sharp debris in a forward arc
  •	Causes lacerations, bleeding, and panic in close-rank units
  •	Radius: ~5–7 feet in front of impact
Tactical Use:
  •	Anti-shield formations or spellcasters
  •	Ideal for breaking defensive lines or clearing narrow chokepoints
  •	Risk of backfire if overheated or jostled—requires careful fletching and transport
