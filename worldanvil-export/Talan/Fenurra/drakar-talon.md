Drakar Talons
“The wall was not a defense. It was a canvas for the Talons.”

These are the signature weapons of Fenurran climbers and shock troops—curved, gauntlet-mounted claws forged from pure Velthite. They are:

• Unbreakable under normal force
• Able to anchor into stone, letting Direwolf riders scale walls mid-charge
• Shaped with draconic elegance, resembling silver-black dragon claws

Only the Draconis know the full forging process, though they trade them with the other tribes freely. Each Talon is built for utility, but every detail—length, curve, grip weight—is personalized to the warrior.

Talons are often passed down, sharpened over generations, engraved with tribal victories or personal shame. Some carry carved words that only the bearer understands.

Purpose:
Hybrid climbing/melee weapon used in high-speed vertical assaults and close-quarters engagement

Construction:
• Forged from refined Velthite—star-metal alloy mined from the Scar of Aeris
• Gauntlet or grip-mounted, with twin or triple claw extensions shaped for grip and gouge
• Surface cooled through heat-tempering to reduce glint and arcane trace

Function:
• Used to scale walls during Hook-Line-Sinker assaults
• Allows mounted warriors to transition from vertical charge to melee strike mid-climb
• In close combat, Talons are capable of puncturing exposed armor joints, throats, or gutlines
• The curved design ensures deep tearing wounds if pulled, not just stabbed

Wielded by:
Talon-climbers, assault specialists, and Direwolf-mounted breachers
Often carried in pairs or combined with short off-hand tools like bone knives or hooks
Not issued en masse—crafted for those trained to use them

Notes:
Some warriors mount fire-starting rods or flares beneath the wrist, used to ignite beacon ropes or trigger Emberframe payloads during scaling actions
In urban combat, Talons can hook into floorboards or ceilings, allowing multi-level attacks or rapid descents
