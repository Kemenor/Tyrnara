**The Hollow of Ten Thousand Threads**

The **Hollow of Ten Thousand Threads** is the lair of the Vermin Queen, one of the Nine Generals of Corruption. It lies deep beneath the earth, a vast cavern system transformed into a hive that swarms with spiders, centipedes, beetles, and other crawling horrors.

The Hollow is named for the endless webs that stretch through its chambers. These strands are not mere silk but strands infused with corruption, glistening with sickly light and strong enough to bind even armored warriors. Whole tunnels are cocooned in silken mesh, forcing intruders to cut their way forward while swarms close in.

**Structure of the Dungeon**

- **The Web-Tunnels**: Narrow passageways coated in sticky threads. They serve as traps and alarm systems, alerting the Queen’s spawn to every intrusion.
- **The Brood Pits**: Cavernous chambers filled with eggs, larvae, and swollen brood-mothers. Adventurers who tarry too long risk being overwhelmed by hatchlings.
- **The Silken Vaults**: Coccooned bodies — mortal victims, beasts, and even adventurers — are suspended here. Some still live, transformed slowly into monstrosities through corruption seeping from the silk.
- **The Throne of the Vermin Queen**: A chamber vast enough to contain her massive form. Draped in layers of living web, she rules here, attended by her chosen spawn — grotesque spider-hybrids once human, now twisted.

**Threats**

- **Endless Swarms**: The Hollow’s strength lies not only in the Vermin Queen herself, but in the unending tide of crawling creatures at her command.
- **Corrupted Silk**: The threads are infused with the god’s power. Weapons and armor entangled in them corrode, and wounds taken while bound are slow to heal.
- **Hive-Mind Influence**: Long exposure to the Hollow’s air fills intruders with whispers, as if the swarm itself seeks to wear down their will and draw them into the hive.

**Significance**

The Hollow of Ten Thousand Threads is a constant source of corruption in the Shadow Steppes. Monsters from the hive burrow outward, seeding lesser nests across the plains. Entire caravans have vanished into web-choked gullies.

Destroying the Vermin Queen would scatter her hive and collapse much of the Hollow — but as long as she endures, the swarms will only grow.
