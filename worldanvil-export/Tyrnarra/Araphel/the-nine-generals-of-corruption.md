**The Nine Generals of Corruption**

The Nine Generals are the foremost servants of the god of Corruption, wrought from his power while he remains bound beneath the earth. Once they were mortal cultists, clerics, beasts, and nameless horrors from the deep places of the world, but each was chosen and remade into a vessel of corruption’s will.

Unlike their imprisoned master, the Nine can be fought — and slain. Their defeat is no small task, for each commands a dungeon of terrible power and legions of corrupted followers. Still, victory is possible, and the fall of a General weakens the Blightfather’s reach in the world above.

**Dungeons of the Nine**

The Nine dwell in colossal subterranean fortresses, known simply as the Nine Dungeons.

These dungeons of did not exist in the long centuries after the Blightfather’s binding. They erupted from the earth eight years ago, vast underground fortresses tearing open pathways into the mortal world. From them came the first great armies of corruption in the modern age.

That first wave was met with fire and steel. The kingdoms united, rallied adventurers and militias, and after bloody campaigns, the hosts of corruption were broken. To prevent another flood, the main entrances of the Nine Dungeons were sealed with wards, stone, and sacrifice.

Yet sealing the gates did not end the threat. The corruption has found new ways to seep upward. Monsters and cultists now emerge through smaller caverns and tunnel networks that lace the land, forcing a constant struggle to contain them.

**The Nine Generals**

- The Vermin Queen
Mistress of swarms. Her hive-dungeon breeds endless spiders, locusts, and crawling things. When her children stir, they pour out in tides that choke entire valleys.
- The Rot-Tyrant
A corpse-warlord clad in ruined armor. His rusting legions march from ossuary-halls, each step spreading despair and decay.
- The Blight-Seer
A prophet twisted into living plague. His labyrinth is filled with pits of contagion, diseased shrines, and clouds of foul miasma.
- The Flesh-Sculptor
A butcher who remakes flesh according to the Blightfather’s designs. His lair is a nightmare of stitched horrors and alchemical vats.
- The Ash-Binder (Defeated)
Once a corrupted elemental beast, dwelling in a volcanic furnace of stone and fire. His defeat is celebrated across the kingdoms as proof that the Nine can fall. Still, his lair remains dangerous, haunted by remnants of his slag-born armies.
- The Whisperer in Dreams
A shadowbound cultist turned nightmare made flesh. His temple of illusions warps dream and waking alike, leaving intruders to wander in endless confusion.
- The Maw Serpent
A colossal, venomous serpent pulled from the world’s depths. Its cavern-dungeon is a network of sinkholes, acid pools, and tunnels large enough to swallow caravans whole.
- The False Saint
Once a holy woman, now a mockery of faith. Her underground chapel oozes false miracles, where prayers rot into curses and the faithful rise as zealots of corruption.
- The Root-Twister
A beast of wild growth remade into a lord of rot. His living dungeon is an underground jungle of strangling vines, carnivorous trees, and choking spores.

**Role in the War of Seals**

The Nine Generals are the corrupted god’s hands in the mortal world. They command armies from their dungeons, spreading corruption through connected caverns and overrun ruins. Each General that falls delays their master’s freedom, but as long as some remain, the Wardstones will never be safe.

The defeat of the Ash-Binder proved the Nine are not invincible, yet eight still remain — and their wrath burns hotter with each loss.
