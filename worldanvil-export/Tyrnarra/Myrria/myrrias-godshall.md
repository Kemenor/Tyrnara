# Myrria's Godshall

**Category:** Myrria  
**Status:** WIP

**Myrria’s Godshall**

The **Godshall of Myrria** is one of the greatest centers of the Adventurers’ Guild in the known world. Carved into the mountainside and rising in tiered halls of blackstone, the Godshall serves as both the heart of the guild’s presence in Myrria and the administrative center for all guild operations across the surrounding kingdoms.

**Architecture and Presence**

The Godshall towers above the Lantern Stairs, its vast facade marked with the **sigil of the Quill & Blade**. The lower levels house the guild’s common services — a tavern and inn for adventurers, offices for quests, a bank hall, and a post station. Above, grand chambers stretch into the mountain itself: libraries, war rooms, armories, and archives of centuries of quests.

At night, enchanted lanterns shimmer along its balconies, casting shifting shadows across the walls. To the people of Myrria, the Godshall is not only an institution but a landmark — a monument of stone and flame that symbolizes safety against the dark.

**Leadership**

The Godshall is led by a **Guild Sovereign**, who holds authority not only over Myrria but also over all guild branches across the Shadow Steppes, Itzasoa, and Ilun Tasun. The Sovereign acts as mediator between kingdoms, guild branches, and the Council of Adventurers that governs Myrria.

Beneath the Sovereign serve **Guildmasters** of major halls, **Head Clerks** of regional offices, and dozens of scribes, brokers, and staff who keep the immense bureaucracy functioning.

**Role in Myrria**

Unlike in many capitals, where the guild works uneasily alongside kings, the Godshall and the Council of Adventurers share power in Myrria. Both institutions overlap: many council members began as guild adventurers, and the Godshall often provides security and resources to the city during times of crisis.

The guild’s neutrality complements Myrria’s ethos as the **City of Second Chances**. Many who arrive as fugitives or exiles join the guild, earning new lives through service and valor. For them, the Godshall is both an employer and a symbol of redemption.

**Regional Importance**

The Godshall of Myrria is not merely local — it is the command center for the guild across multiple realms. It coordinates defenses against the Nine Generals, dispatches expeditions to dungeons, and organizes relief for border towns plagued by corruption. Its magical ledgers serve as the central hub for the guild bank across the region, and its couriers carry sealed missives even into warzones.

When the **Grand Assembly** is called, the Guild Sovereign of Myrria always attends, their voice carrying the weight of entire kingdoms.

**Inside the Godshall of Myrria**

Stepping through the great shadow-carved archway of the Godshall, a visitor is immediately enveloped in the **living heart of the Adventurers’ Guild**. The entry hall is a broad chamber of blackstone polished smooth, its high ceiling supported by columns engraved with the guild’s motto: *“Quill and Blade, One Purpose.”*

**The Entry Hall**

- **The Postboard**: A massive wall of quest-slips dominates the left side of the hall, layered with notices ranging from simple escort requests to dangerous monster hunts. Adventurers crowd here constantly, plucking postings, arguing over rights, or tacking up personal notes.
- **Grouping Tables**: Broad wooden tables sit before the postboard, where adventurers gather in noisy clusters, negotiating who will form a party for a quest. Chalk marks on the tables list postings by rank (Bronze to Starsteel).
- **The Guild Counter**: Along the right wall, a line of guild clerks manage contracts, verify quest completion, and dispense rewards. Behind them, shelves of enchanted ledgers glow faintly with synchronized records.

**Services Wing**

Opposite the counter stands the **Guild Bank and Post Office**:

- **Bank Hall**: A reinforced chamber with rune-carved vault doors. Magical ledgers hover above the counters, synchronizing balances with every branch in Araphel.
- **Post Station**: A smaller office staffed with scribes and couriers, sorting sealed letters stamped with the guild’s sigil before dispatching them across the region.

**The Adventurers’ Library**

Tucked along the western wing of the ground floor lies the **Adventurers’ Library**, a space open to all guild members regardless of rank.

- **Contents**: Journals of past expeditions, bestiaries of monsters, maps of local terrain, and practical manuals on survival, combat, and spellwork.
- **Atmosphere**: Unlike the vaulted Lantern Archive, this library is lively — adventurers swap stories, scribble notes into open ledgers, and scrawl warnings about dungeons and monsters for future parties.
- **Keeper**: The library is managed by a **Guild Scribe**, often a retired adventurer, who ensures books are copied, replaced, and annotated for accuracy.

It is said that more quests are prepared here, over maps and scribbled notes, than at the quest tables themselves.

**The Taproom**

From deeper within the hall comes the sound of **merry laughter, clinking tankards, and bard-song**. The **Taproom of the Godshall** is a sprawling tavern reserved for adventurers, though locals with business in the guild sometimes slip inside. Its walls are hung with monster trophies — not as private decorations, but as public reminders of what the guild has accomplished.

The taproom also houses:

- **The Hearth**: A great firepit, ringed by seats where adventurers swap tales and scars.
- **The Trophy Wall**: Heads, claws, and relics from monsters slain in Araphel, some donated by Seraphel herself.
- **Sleeping Quarters**: Dormitories above the taproom provide simple bunks for low-ranked adventurers, while private rooms can be rented by higher ranks.

**Deeper Halls (Restricted)**

Beyond the common areas lie the **restricted wings** of the Godshall:

- **The Hall of Quests**: Where high-rank contracts are assigned and raid-plans against the Nine are drawn.
- **The Lantern Archive**: A sealed library of maps, journals, and accounts of adventurers past, restricted to Mithral rank and above.
- **War Vaults**: Armories stocked with enchanted weapons and emergency supplies, accessible only in times of crisis.
- **Council Chamber**: The meeting place of the Guild Sovereign and her advisors, carved high into the mountain’s spine.
