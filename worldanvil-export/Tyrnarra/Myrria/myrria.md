**Myrria**

**Myrria** is an independent city-state, nestled in a crescent of mountains and known as the **City of Second Chances**. It is both a sanctuary and a fortress, its streets carved into blackstone terraces that climb the slopes and wind into hidden strongholds. At its heart lies the **Sanctum of Veils**, where the God of Shadows resides and where the seventh Wardstone rests.

**Geography and Structure**

The city is cradled by mountain ridges, with its districts built into the slopes and cliffsides. Narrow bridges and winding stairways connect terraces, while tunnels and fortified passages burrow deep into the rock. Myrria does not end at its walls — whole wings of the city extend *inside* the mountains, forming hidden strongholds and vault-like sanctuaries.

At night, pale lanterns shine from both cliffside windows and mountain halls, giving the city the appearance of a starlit constellation scattered across stone.

**Government and Society**

Though the **God of Shadows** resides within his great temple, he does not rule the city directly. Authority rests with the **Council of Adventurers**, a body of seasoned wanderers and heroes who once lived by the sword but now guide Myrria’s fate.

The city welcomes all who seek redemption or rebirth. In Myrria, names can be left behind, past crimes set aside, and even the most hunted soul may find refuge — so long as they abide by the Council’s decrees. This is why it is called the City of Second Chances.

**The Divine Wardstone**

The Wardstone of Myrria rests in the Sanctum of Veils. It glows only beneath the night sky, its light dimmed at midday as though hidden from the sun. The faithful believe that as long as the Wardstone endures, the shadows of the god will guard the city from corruption.

**Role in the War of Seals**

Myrria is not bound to any kingdom, yet it is indispensable. Its Wardstone is one of the seven anchors that hold the god of Corruption beneath the earth. Were Myrria to fall, the entire chain would be imperiled. For this reason, even rival kingdoms respect its independence, knowing that the survival of all rests partly in the mountain city.

**Districts and Strongholds of Myrria**

Myrria sprawls across mountain terraces and tunnels carved deep into stone. Its districts rise one above the other, linked by stairways, bridges, and hidden passages. Each has its own character, yet all are bound together under the lantern glow of the God of Shadows.

**The Lantern Stairs**

The first sight most travelers see: a great stairway that winds up the slope, flanked by hundreds of pale lanterns. Pilgrims ascend barefoot to honor the God of Shadows, leaving tokens of their old lives along the way. The stairway district is lined with hostels, shrines, and markets.

**Cliffside Bazaar**

Built on wide terraces overlooking the valleys below, the Bazaar is alive with merchants, traders, and smugglers. Here, anyone can sell their wares — no questions asked. Exotic goods flow in from all kingdoms, and even fugitives can find trade here, provided they pay the tolls.

**The Veiled Quarter**

The city’s main residential district, where those who seek new lives settle. Streets are narrow, often draped in dark silks and banners. Names are often changed here, and neighbors rarely ask about past lives. Many inns and taverns thrive, providing anonymity for adventurers.

**The Sanctum of Veils**

The central temple of the God of Shadows and the resting place of the **Divine Wardstone**. Its walls are veiled with black and silver silks, said to shift like living shadows. The innermost sanctum is open only to priests, the Council of Adventurers, and those the god himself summons.

**Shadowguard Keep**

The martial heart of the city. The Shadowguard are elite warriors sworn to defend Myrria, not by lineage but by oath. The Keep is part fortress, part barracks, and part training ground. From here, patrols watch both the mountain passes and the city’s underways.

**The Hidden Vaults**

Tunnels and chambers carved deep within the mountains. Some are used for storage and safekeeping, while others hold secrets even the Council guards closely. Whispers say entire vaults are filled with relics of adventurers who died seeking redemption — or with prisoners too dangerous to release.

**The Blackstone Stronghold**

An ancient citadel built into the highest ridge, its towers emerging from the peaks like jagged fangs. Once a fortress of war, it now houses the Council of Adventurers. From here they deliberate, passing laws and guiding the city’s fate. Many halls are also dedicated to training new adventurers who seek their second chance.

**The Starlight Cliffs**

The uppermost terraces, where lanterns are replaced by open skies. At night, gatherings are held here, celebrating festivals of shadow and rebirth. It is here that the Divine Wardstone shines most brightly, reflecting the stars.
