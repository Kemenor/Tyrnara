# **Catjomin Sake**

- **Type:** Fine Spirit
- **Origin:** Brewed exclusively by kitsune master-brewers from their ancestral homeland (TBD), Catjomin Sake is more than a beverage — it is a sacred tradition, a test of spiritual mastery, and a vessel of emotion. Brewing is a rite passed down through generations, and the craft is deeply tied to the number of tails — and thus, the power — a kitsune possesses.
- **Description:** A pale, golden sake with smooth, floral notes and a warm, lingering magical resonance. The flavor dances on the tongue, shifting subtly with each stripe level. Drinkers often report vivid dreams, emotional clarity, and an eerie sense of shared memory.
- **Potency:** Deceptively delicate. One cup loosens the tongue. Two opens the heart. The third may unseal the soul.

**Cultural Significance**

- Offering Catjomin Sake is a gesture of **profound respect** — or **romantic intent** if offered privately.
- Sharing a bottle with more than four stripes is seen as a **bond of trust or affection**.
- Among kitsune, **refusing a shared glass** without sacred reason (mourning, penance, or divine vow) is a **serious insult**.
- The **number of stripes** is never spoken aloud — doing so is considered gauche. True connoisseurs recognize the tier at a glance.

## **Catjomin Sake – The Nine Tails of the Brew**
Each bottle of Catjomin Sake bears **1 to 9 stripes**, marking the number of tails the kitsune brewer possessed at the time of creation. These stripes are not just marks of power — they are a sacred taxonomy, each one with its own **poetic name**, **social use**, and **emotional weight**.
