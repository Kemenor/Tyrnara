**The Menagerie**

**Overview**

The **Menagerie** is the Red Empire’s most secretive and reviled corps: an order of war-mages, alchemists, necromancers and surgeons dedicated to the perfection of the mortal form. They do not view themselves as creators of monsters, but as artisans of war, forging weapons in flesh and blood. To them, every scar is data, every scream a step closer to removing weakness. Flesh, bone, and even the soul are clay to be reforged. No law, no god, no grave is sacred if it hinders progress.

Their goal is not chaos, but **progress **— the elimination of frailty, the forging of mortals into something beyond gods.

**Philosophy**

- Their creed: weakness is the greatest sin, and all things — even death — must be overcome
- The Menagerie believe every flaw — fear, hunger, fatigue, even mortality itself — can be stripped away, leaving only perfected will housed in perfected flesh.
- They reject notions of monstrosity: if a creation is effective, then it is beautiful.
- Failure is not tragedy, but proof of a path not worth pursuing.

**Organization**

- **The Master Curator**
  - The highest authority, chosen by consensus of peers, not appointment.
  - Seen as the “keeper of the collection,” directing the Menagerie’s projects.
- **Curators**
  - Senior war-mages, alchemists, and surgeons who lead specific lines of experimentation.
  - Each keeps their own “catalogue” — a collection of theories, failures, and living subjects.
- **Keepers**
  - Mid-rank practitioners tasked with maintaining laboratories, overseeing Fodder test subjects, and implementing the Curators’ designs.
- **Collectors**
  - Agents sent into the Empire (and beyond) to gather subjects, materials, or rare reagents. They are trained to disappear into any society.

**Methods**

- **Surgical Reforging:** Cutting, grafting, and restructuring the body with steel, bone, or scale.
- **Alchemical Transmutation:** Flooding veins with concoctions that twist organs, harden flesh, or fuel violent strength.
- **Arcane Infusion:** Branding runes into skin, weaving enchantments into marrow, or stitching spirits into flesh.
- **Necromantic Reclamation **– tearing knowledge from the dead, enslaving corpses for experiments, or stitching soul-fragments into living hosts.
- **Conditioning Trials:** Survivors of starvation, fire, venom, or battle are mapped for insight — “the empire learns most from those who almost die, but do not.”

**Creations**

- **Augments
**Soldiers reinforced with steel-bone, hardened skin, or heightened senses. Practical enhancements, subtle enough to blend into rank and file.
- **Exemplars
**Attempts to refine whole cohorts of soldiers: fearless, sleepless, tireless, obedient.
- **The Apex Line
**Singular predators reforged to embody terror and efficiency. Designed to be unleashed as living weapons of annihilation.
- **Ashborn
**Fodder or Broken reforged by necromancy, shuffling engines of endurance. Living parts stitched together with the dead, sustained by arcane bindings.
- **Harvested
**The corpses of fallen enemies, reanimated and stripped of individuality. War-tools of bone and sinew, fielded in masses.
- **Fleshwarps
**Twisted survivors of the Menagerie’s most unstable experiments.
  - **Origins:** Some are deliberate projects, others “accidents” too useful to discard.
  - **Form:** Their bodies writhe with mismatched limbs, fused organs, or features grafted from beasts. No two are alike.
  - **Role:** They are deployed as shock troops, terror weapons, or laborers in environments lethal to unaltered humans.
  - **Status:** Considered both failure *and* asset — not the perfected ideal, but a reminder that even flawed flesh can be made to serve the Empire’s will.

**Reputation**

- **Inside the Empire:**
  - Feared but tolerated.
  - The Blooded call them “Fleshwrights,” half-respect, half-revulsion.
  - The Forged sometimes envy them as “the pinnacle of craftsmanship,” but few openly admit it.
- **Outside the Empire:**
  - The Menagerie is whispered like a curse. Parents threaten children with “the Collectors” coming in the night.
  - Captives of war fear them more than execution.

**Symbols & Culture**

- **Symbol:** A crimson serpent devouring its own tail, set inside a cage of black iron bars.
- **Colors:** White and blue uniforms (laboratory purity, clinical detachment) worn over hidden crimson linings — a reminder that their work is soaked in blood.
- **Motto: ***“Perfection has no pity.”*
- **Rituals:** Every Curator keeps a personal “collection room” — shelves of preserved organs, bones, or failed grafts, reminders of the paths discarded.
