**The Red Empire**

**Overview**

The **Red Empire** is a vast, authoritarian state forged in fire and blood. It stretches across fertile plains, river valleys, and deserts, unified not by faith or heritage but by **the creed of mortal supremacy**. The Empire rejects all gods, declaring them illusions or parasites feeding on the weak. To the Red, only through discipline, hardship, and sacrifice can humanity ascend to its rightful destiny — masters of themselves, masters of the world.

**Ruler**

- **The Crimson Emperor**
  - Supreme commander and living symbol of the Empire’s ideals.
  - Said to have endured *The Ordeal of Ash* — forty days of isolation, starvation, and pain — emerging scarred but unbroken, proving that a mortal will can outlast any divine trial.
  - His throne, the **Pyre Seat**, is forged from weapons of defeated kings, melted together in conquest.

**Military**

The Empire is built on its military — every citizen a resource, every victory a lesson.

- **Red Army (Legions of Flame)**
  - Citizen-soldiers, bound by lifelong service.
  - Organized into legions, each a self-sufficient war machine of infantry, engineers, and war-mages.
  - Every legion drills daily not just in combat but in endurance trials: fasting, exposure, forced marches, all meant to burn away weakness.
- **Ashen Guard**
  - The Emperor’s chosen elite, drawn from those who survive the harshest trials.
  - Their armor is blackened from ritual fires they walk through to prove their worth.
  - Serve as both enforcers of imperial law and executioners of failed officers.
- **The Menagerie**
  - A secretive corps of war-mages, alchemists, and surgeons.
  - Their creed is *“If gods would make monsters, then so shall we — and better.”*
  - Their work creates horrors like Kemenor: mortals reforged through agony into apex predators.
- **Iron Tide (Navy)**
  - Fleet of black-keeled warships, sails dyed crimson.
  - Specialize in coastal annihilation — burning ports to cinders before annexing them.
  - Captains are expected to scuttle their own ships before surrender; survival is shame.

**Society & Creed**

- **Godless Philosophy:** Religion is outlawed. Priests and temples are dismantled or repurposed into academies and drill yards. To worship gods is seen as weakness — surrendering one’s will to an external master.
- **Children & Trials:** At age ten, children are subjected to the **Trial of Ash**. Those who endure join academies to be shaped into soldiers, engineers, or overseers. Those who fail become laborers or are cast aside.
- **Hierarchy:** Nobility exists only by merit — generals, governors, scholars rise through discipline, not birth. Even family ties mean nothing without achievement.
- **Law:** Mercy is weakness; justice is survival. Theft, cowardice, or treason are punishable by execution, often public, as reminders that weakness breeds decay.

**Symbols & Culture**

- **Colors:** Crimson and black — blood spilled willingly, ash left by fire.
- **Emblem:** A phoenix without wings, rising not from fire but from sharpened steel — symbolizing rebirth by mortal hands, not divine grace.
- **Motto: ***“Through hardship, dominion. Through discipline, eternity.”*
- **Festivals:** Marked by displays of strength: mass endurance marches, public duels, communal fasts, and the breaking of captured enemies in the arenas.

**The Red Empire – Social Hierarchy**

**1. The Pyre Throne**

- The Crimson Emperor sits alone at the top — not divine, but the mortal ideal realized.
- He is seen as the embodiment of the creed: unbending, disciplined, enduring.
- His word is absolute law, but his legitimacy is tied to his proven strength; weakness would shatter his rule.

**2. The Command Caste**

- Generals, Legates, and High Strategoi of the Red Army.
- Masters of the Ashen Guard and overseers of The Menagerie.
- Their status is tied directly to military victories, endurance in trials, and ability to command loyalty.
- Scholars of war and discipline who fail in battle are stripped of rank, often denoted to the rank of Fodder

**3. The Forged**

- Esteemed artisans, engineers, researchers, and innovators who “forge strength from weakness.”
- Blacksmiths, siege engineers, alchemists, and Menagerie scholars fall here.
- They are celebrated when their work advances the Empire — a master armor-smith may outrank a common officer.
- Their worth is measured in results, not intention
- Their creed: “To craft is to conquer the world’s resistance.”

**4. The Blooded**

- Professional soldiers of the Red Army legions.
- The backbone of the Empire, respected for their endurance and loyalty.
- Granted stipends, land rights, and the right to bear crimson cloaks — symbols of belonging to the Army.
- Cowardice in battle demotes them instantly to Fodder.

**5. The Respected Hands**

- Common craftsmen and skilled laborers — carpenters, masons, shipwrights, healers, scribes.
- Not warriors, but useful and steady.
- Their respect comes from productivity and discipline, with guilds organized around military-style hierarchies.

**6. The Ashen**

- Unskilled laborers — farmhands, dockworkers, miners.
- Necessary for the Empire’s survival but seen as replaceable.
- They live hard lives, fed and housed as needed, no luxuries, with opportunities to rise through proving themselves in the Trial of Ash or volunteering for soldier conscription.

**7. The Broken**

- The lowest rung — the needy, the unproductive, and the “weak.”
- Beggars, cripples, the elderly without family, and those who fail the Trial of Ash but are not strong enough even for menial work.
- They are often pressed into dangerous state projects (quarry work, war-mines) where survival is rare.
- The Empire sees them not as citizens but as burdens to be burned away.

**Special Note: Fodder**

- Fodder is not a caste but a punishment.
- Those demoted here are given whatever equipment is left over, and thrown into the frontlines as living shields or used as first charge in a suicide mission .
- If they endure, they are permitted to rise again, often into the Ashen caste, and may climb from there.
- The role is both punishment and test: failure is death, survival is proof of worth.

**Cultural Philosophy of Mobility**

- Mobility is real and constant. Rise and fall are part of the Empire’s rhythm.
- Movement down is not branded as shame but accepted as a mortal being “placed into their correct station.”
- Movement up is a reward and honor, earned only by endurance, discipline, or proving strength.
- This constant testing keeps the Empire’s hierarchy fluid, but always tied to merit through ordeal.
