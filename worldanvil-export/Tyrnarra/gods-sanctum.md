**The Grand Gods in the Mortal World**

**The Living Sanctums**

- Each Grand God resides in a **Sanctum**, housed within a monumental temple at the heart of their free city-state.
- These sanctums are not merely symbolic shrines — they are **literal homes**. Within their walls, the gods eat, sleep, dream, and walk among mortals.
- While their true power is beyond mortal comprehension, the Grand Gods have chosen **physical, enduring forms** on the Life Layer, allowing them to live *within* the world they once ruled.

**Presence in Daily Life**

- Citizens of the free cities may **see their god with their own eyes** — walking through gardens, attending ceremonies, or even speaking in public forums.
- Though bound by the God’s Law not to rule mortals directly, the gods’ very existence shapes the culture, politics, and identity of their city-states.
- A merchant in Jianna’s city may claim she once smiled at him in passing — and that alone can be enough to raise a family’s fortunes.
- Farmers in Sarrum’s city say the god himself walks the fields before planting season, ensuring the earth is ready.

**The Council of Thirteen**

- When they gather (a rare event), the Grand Gods meet **in person** — no avatars, no visions.
- The full Council of Thirteen assembling is a moment of awe and terror for mortals, a sign that history itself is about to turn.
- Yet even in council, they bicker, debate, and stall; they are gods, but also beings of personality, flaw, and contradiction.

**The Weight of Proximity**

- To live in a city of the divine is both blessing and burden.
  - Blessing: miracles may be witnessed, wisdom freely offered, protection assured.
  - Burden: the god’s flaws manifest just as tangibly. Komo’s city often suffers destructive fires. Forseti’s city drowns in red tape. Vesuna’s streets may shift overnight.
- The people of these cities develop **intense loyalty** — both to their god and to the identity shaped by divine presence.

**The Mortal View**

- For mortals outside the free cities, the Grand Gods feel *distant but undeniable*, like mountains on the horizon.
- For those inside, they are **living myths**, whose footsteps echo in temple halls and whose decisions ripple across generations.
