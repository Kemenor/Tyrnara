# **The Spider’s Silk Inn**
**Type:** Tavern, Neutral Ground, Information Hub

**Location:** Crossroads, a bustling trade nexus where multiple territories meet

**Proprietor:** Matron Charna, ancient Anadi

**Overview**

The Spider’s Silk Inn is less an inn and more the **neutral heart of Crossroads**.

Within its walls, guild laws, noble edicts, and even kingdom-level rivalries are suspended.

- **Matron Charna’s authority** is absolute, enforced by her web of favors, quiet leverage, and the unspoken threat of her own considerable power.
- It is at once an **information exchange**, **mercenary hiring hall**, and **sanctuary** for those needing to disappear.
- The inn’s name comes from the **enchanted silver silk** woven throughout its frame, which:
  - **Dampens hostile magic**
  - **Lets Charna perceive all activity under her roof**

**Atmosphere:**

- **Scent:** Crisp hops and spiced cider cut through richer notes of venison stew and fresh-baked rye bread.
- **Sight:** Silver spider-silk strands glimmer in the rafters, catching the glow of floating light orbs drifting lazily between tables. Waitstaff descend gracefully on silk rappel lines, trays balanced in one hand as they deliver steaming tankards and heaping platters.

**The Matron – Charna**

- **Race:** Anadi (spiderfolk)
- **Appearance:** Ebony exoskeleton with a mother-of-pearl sheen, eight glittering eyes, slender limbs moving with slow precision.
- **Manner:** Watchful, deliberate, faintly amused — rarely descends from her vantage point.
- **Seat of Power:** A nest-like platform suspended at the center of the ceiling, from which she oversees the entire inn.
- **Reputation:**
  - Knows the debts, secrets, and weaknesses of half the region.
  - Keeps a flawless memory of every deal struck under her roof.
  - Said to have woven every enchanted thread in the building herself.

**Notable Features**

- **Enchanted Silver Silk:** Threaded through the rafters and walls, it suppresses aggressive magic and extends Charna’s awareness.
- **The Hiring Wall:** A noticeboard covered in contracts, bounties, and discreet arrangements.
- **The Webroom:** A curtained upper chamber for private negotiations and sensitive meetings, accessible only by silk lines.
- **Silk Lines Service:** Staff — Anadi and nimble humanoids — move between floor and rafters via silk rappel lines, allowing swift service in the busiest hours.
- **Guest Rooms:** Private, well-appointed chambers available for rest and recovery. Charna’s webs seal each room, muffling all sound and preventing magical or mundane eavesdropping. These wards make the rooms highly sought after by travelers with dangerous enemies — or dangerous secrets.

**Common Patrons**

- **Mercenaries & Adventurers:** Seeking work, contacts, or sanctuary.
- **Merchants:** Closing sensitive deals or finding escorts for risky routes.
- **Informants & Spies:** Trading in coin and information alike.
- **Runaways & Exiles:** Sheltering under Charna’s neutrality until they can move on.

**Rumors & Hooks**

- A kingdom’s missing emissary was last seen entering the Spider’s Silk — and never left.
- The silver silk isn’t just magic-dampening; it may also be *recording*.
- Once in a generation, Charna offers “The Favor” to a guest — and those who accept rise to power with her unseen help.
- A forgotten upper room has been sealed in silk for decades; no one but Charna is allowed near it.

Description given by a regular:
"The Spider’s Silk," he began, "is an inn quite unlike any other. The first thing you’ll notice is the sound. The common room is alive — always alive. And in the center, on the stage, is where it all happens: concerts that fill the rafters with song, arm wrestling matches that shake the tables, storytellers weaving their craft, and the occasional foolish competition that draws cheers and groans in equal measure. They say the Spider’s Silk never rests."

He shifted, letting the next detail roll out like the scent itself.

"Then the smell — crisp hops and spiced cider cut through the richer notes of venison stew and fresh-baked rye bread. The food is good. The drink… better. And if the whispers are true, the cellar holds a bottle of every spirit, wine, and ale ever brewed in the known world."

A small smile tugged at his lips as his voice softened.

"And then — the sight. Down on the floor, the chaos of life: laughter, dice clattering, the flash of coin. But above?" His eyes seemed to lift to some imagined ceiling. "Filigree beauty. Silver spider-silk strands glimmering in the rafters, catching the soft glow of floating light orbs that drift lazily between tables. And then movement — graceful and precise. The waitstaff descend on silk rappel lines, trays balanced effortlessly in one hand, delivering steaming tankards and laden platters to waiting guests without spilling so much as a drop."

He let a pause settle, his gaze fixing just above the listener’s head.

"And if you follow those lines upward… your eyes will be drawn to the very heart of it all. A nest-like platform at the ceiling’s center — and from it, eight eyes staring back at you. Lady Charna, the Matron. Ancient. Immaculate. And it is said," he lowered his voice, "there is not a whisper under her roof she does not hear."

The warmth of his smile didn’t change, but there was steel in his words as he went on.

"Even with all that chaos, you’ll never see a brawl here. Her authority is absolute. To the sides, the corners are reserved for alcoves — pools of shadow and privacy where low voices carry no further than the table, perfect for conversations not meant to be overheard. But if your dealings need true secrecy, the Webrooms are open to those of sufficient means. Rooms sealed with her silk — no sound escapes, no magic intrudes. Secrets sleep safely there."

Then he leaned forward, voice dipping into something conspiratorial.

"It’s the perfect place to hide your secrets… or to learn just how much they are worth in gold."
