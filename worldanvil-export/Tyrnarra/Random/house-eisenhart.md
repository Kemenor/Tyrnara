# **House Eisenhart**

- **Type:** Noble House (Mostly Dwarven)
- **Origin:** One of the most prestigious bloodlines in the **Order of Steam**, the dwarven kingdom renowned for fusing engineering genius with arcane precision.
- **Specialization:** Master crafters of high-grade military equipment and large-scale war machines, including **armored airships**, **steam-driven siege engines**, and **arcane-powered artillery**.
- **Reputation:** Known for uncompromising quality, functional yet imposing designs, and weapons that remain operational for centuries. Their work is as much a status symbol as a tool of war.
- **Political Influence:**
  - Holds several hereditary seats on the Council of the Forge, giving them significant sway over the kingdom’s military policy and industrial priorities.
  - Maintains long-term supply contracts with multiple foreign powers — though they rarely sell their most advanced designs beyond the Order of Steam’s borders.
- **Motto: ***“Strength Forged in Honor.”*
- **Notable Exports:** The *Eisenhart Leviathan*-class warship, the *Ironwing* line of heavy combat airships, and precision-forged runeblade bayonets favored by elite dwarven regiments.

**Highforge Matriarch: Tharka Eisenhart**

- **Race:** Mountain Dwarf
- **Age:** 217
- **Titles:** Lady of the Anvil Throne, Warden of the Steel Marches, Keeper of the Furnace-Law
- **Profile:**
  - Stern, unsmiling, and intensely pragmatic, Tharka leads House Eisenhart like she would lead a war campaign: with ruthless efficiency and a zero-tolerance policy for incompetence.
  - Famous for personally field-testing new weapons in live combat scenarios — and once silencing a noble critic by hurling a prototype cannonball across a boardroom table.
  - Wears an adaptive exo-frame — part armor, part medical support — crafted after surviving an assassination attempt that left her with lasting spinal injuries.
- **Politics:**
  - Deeply loyal to the Order of Steam but distrusts its softer political wings.
  - Pushes aggressively for military modernization and limited foreign expansion.
  - Openly opposed to selling Eisenhart technology to “unproven hands,” even among allies.

**Eisenhart-Class War Relic: *****Stahlglanz***

- **Type:** Mobile Fortress (Land & Sea)
- **Designation: ***Stahlglanz* — “Steelshine” in Old Dwarvish
- **Dimensions:** Over 400 meters long, built like a floating citadel with retractable wheels and submersible hull segments.
- **Mobility:** Moves across land via arcane-driven tread assemblies and powered legs on the front half, while its rear section reconfigures for aquatic propulsion, enabling sea traversal.
- **Defenses:**
  - Reinforced Ethersteel plating laced with magical wards against elemental and arcane assault
  - Shield-array projectors powered by a mana furnace the size of a cathedral
  - Full battery of heavy rune cannons and long-range siege mortars
- **Features:**
  - Holds a barracks, hangar bays for Eisenhart-class Ironwing airships, and a command throne interfaced with the ship’s engine spirit
  - A mobile forge-deck staffed by golem-assisted engineers for on-the-field repairs and weapon production
- **Historical Role:**
  - Built during the *Siege of Nine Storms*, where it broke the blockade of three enemy nations by walking straight through the sea and leveling a fleet from beneath.
  - Since then, it is only deployed when the **Order of Steam** declares *Total Mobilization* — something that has not happened in over a century.
