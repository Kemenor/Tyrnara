# Adventurers Guild

**Type:** Organization  
**Category:** Adventurer Guild  
**Status:** WIP  
**Updated:** 2025-09-06

---

**The Adventurers’ Guild**

The **Adventurers’ Guild** is a worldwide organization dedicated to protecting mortals from the threats of monsters, corruption, and the unknown. From sprawling capitals to isolated frontier towns, guild branches can be found in nearly every corner of the world.

The guild acts as the link between common folk and adventurers, organizing requests, assigning missions, and ensuring that those who risk their lives in the dark receive recognition and reward.

**Purpose and Duties**

The guild exists as the first and often only shield between mortals and the dangers that lurk beyond their walls. Its accepted duties include:

- **Monster Hunting**: Tracking, fighting, and eliminating dangerous beasts and corrupted creatures.
- **Anomaly Investigation**: Exploring ruins, uncovering magical disturbances, and studying phenomena that threaten mortal settlements.
- **Protection and Escort**: Safeguarding trade routes, pilgrimages, and expeditions into perilous lands.
- **Dungeon Raids**: Coordinating large-scale expeditions into corrupted strongholds such as the Nine Dungeons.

The guild accepts all such quests, whether they come from peasants, kings, or even other guilds.

**Neutrality**

The Adventurers’ Guild maintains strict political neutrality. Its branches exist in free democracies, merchant republics, and even iron-fisted dictatorships. The guild does not take sides in wars, coups, or mortal disputes. Its focus is singular: **to combat threats against mortal existence itself.**

This stance often brings tension with rulers who expect loyalty, but the guild’s reputation and necessity keep it tolerated — even respected — in nearly every nation.

**Ranks of the Guild**

Adventurers are ranked according to their deeds and capabilities, not by wealth or heritage. Rank determines what quests an adventurer may accept, and higher ranks carry the burden of greater responsibility.

- **Bronze** — Novices and initiates. Bronze adventurers take on minor tasks: hunting pests, escorting merchants, and clearing small dens.
- **Silver** — Reliable adventurers trusted to face dangerous beasts, scout ruins, and act as guards on longer journeys.
- **Gold** — Seasoned fighters and spellcasters. Gold adventurers are often given authority over small groups and are sent against serious threats such as trolls, wyverns, or large-scale anomalies.
- **Platinum** — Veterans of renown. Platinum adventurers handle high-risk threats that could endanger towns or regions. They may lead raids into major dungeons.
- **Mithral** — Rare masters of the craft, often known by name across kingdoms. They stand against catastrophic threats: corrupted beasts, giant warbands, or incursions from the Nine Dungeons.
- **Orichalcum** — Legendary heroes, few in number. Their quests often decide the fate of nations. The fall of the Ash-Binder was led by Orichalcum-ranked adventurers.
- **Starsteel** — The highest honor of the guild, bestowed only upon those whose deeds rival myth. Starsteel adventurers are symbols as much as warriors, living legends entrusted with the defense of the mortal world itself.

**Organization of the Adventurers’ Guild**

The Adventurers’ Guild is a worldwide network with branches ranging from lone clerks in remote villages to monumental halls in divine cities. Its structure allows it to serve both frontier farmers and kings, uniting adventurers under a single banner while remaining politically neutral.

**Branch Office**

The smallest and most common form of guild presence.

- Found in small towns, villages, or remote frontiers.
- Staff may be as little as a single **Guild Clerk**, or even part-time assistance in isolated settlements.
- Handles simple contracts: pest control, local patrols, and escort missions.
- Reports to a larger Guild Office or Guildhall in a nearby city.

**Guild Office**

The standard guild presence in larger towns.

- Staffed by multiple clerks and scribes.
- Led by a **Head Clerk**, though some larger offices may be overseen by a **Guildmaster**.
- Processes higher-level contracts, organizes teams, and serves as the hub for nearby Branch Offices.

**Guildhall**

The iconic form of the guild, found in major cities.

- A large office complex with its own **inn, tavern, and sleeping quarters** for adventurers.
- Always led by a **Guildmaster**.
- Acts as both an adventurer’s home and a public face of the guild, often serving as neutral ground for adventurers of all creeds.

**Kingshall**

The grand guild centers of capital cities.

- Larger and more influential than standard guildhalls.
- Led by a **Grand Guildmaster**.
- Serves as the primary liaison between the guild and royal courts, while still upholding neutrality.
- Oversees all Guildhalls and Offices within the kingdom.

**Godshall**

The highest-ranking regional guild centers, found only in **city-states of divine significance** such as Myrria.

- Monumental in size and influence, responsible for all guild activity across multiple kingdoms.
- Led by a **Guild Sovereign**.
- Houses archives of adventurer records, ancient relics, and high councils of adventurers who advise both kings and temples.

**The Grand Assembly**

The supreme council of the Adventurers’ Guild.

- Not a permanent location, but a **gathering of all Grand Guildmasters and Guild Sovereigns**.
- Convened only in times of great crisis or to resolve disputes of policy.
- From among themselves, they elect the **First of the Quill & Blade**, the highest authority in the guild.
- The First is not a monarch but a figure of respect, chosen for wisdom, valor, and their ability to balance words (*the Quill*) with deeds (*the Blade*).

**Additional Services of the Adventurers’ Guild**

While the Adventurers’ Guild was founded to combat monsters and protect mortals, its global reach has given rise to two essential services that now serve adventurers and commonfolk alike.

**The Guild Bank**

Originally established so adventurers could deposit their treasure rather than carry heavy and dangerous coin purses into dungeons, the **Guild Bank** has since expanded into one of the most trusted financial networks in the world.

- **Magical Ledgers**: Each guild office maintains enchanted ledgers that synchronize balances across the world. An adventurer may deposit coin in one Guildhall and withdraw it in another, no matter the distance.
- **Public Access**: Once restricted to adventurers, the bank is now open to merchants, nobles, and commonfolk. In some regions, the Guild Bank rivals or even replaces royal treasuries.
- **Neutral Trust**: Because of the guild’s political neutrality, kingdoms often rely on the Guild Bank to hold funds during times of war or unrest, trusting that their enemies cannot touch it.

**The Guild Post**

The Adventurers’ Guild also serves as the world’s most reliable courier network.

- **Remote Reach**: Because the guild maintains offices even in frontier villages, letters and parcels can be carried to places where no other service exists.
- **Adventurer Couriers**: In dangerous regions, adventurers themselves may be hired to deliver letters, ensuring messages can travel through lands plagued by monsters.
- **Sealed by the Guild**: All official Guild Post is sealed with a magical mark, guaranteeing authenticity and protecting against tampering.

Together, these services make the Adventurers’ Guild more than a shield against monsters: it has become a backbone of global stability, tying kingdoms, cities, and villages together through coin and communication.
