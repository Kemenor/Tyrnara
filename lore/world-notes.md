# World Notes — Tyrnarra Lore Reference

A running file for complex, easy-to-misremember lore facts. Read this before writing anything about cosmology, the gods, or history.

---

## The Week of Crimson Rain (0 GR / 0 MR)

- It was not a conflict between a small group of gods — **hundreds of gods** were present on the Material Plane during the Gods' Era, and the Week of Crimson Rain involved casualties among divine and mortal alike at an unprecedented scale.
- Cronus ended it by proposing the **Gods' Law** — a binding compact. Gods willing to accept it stayed. Gods unwilling to be bound withdrew to the other planes (Prelife or Postlife) or were destroyed in the chaos.
- **More gods could have stayed** — the thirteen were not the only survivors, just the only ones who chose to accept the binding.
- The thirteen who stayed are the 13 Bound Gods. The rest — the vast majority — withdrew, taking their own pantheons with them. **Other pantheons exist** on other planes (see below).

---

## Other Pantheons

- Many gods beyond the 13 exist in Prelife (Layer 1) and Postlife (Layer 3).
- Elven ancestral pantheons, deep god cults, demon lord worship, and others all have real divine power and active churches in Talan.
- The 13 are distinctive because they are *present on the Material Plane* and *bound by the Gods' Law* — not because they are the only gods that exist.
- A note in the Talan primer (Magic & Faith tab) already references this: "Elven ancestral pantheons, deep god cults, demon lord worship — all have churches and real divine power flowing through them."

---

## The Gods' Origins

- **None of the 13 have explained their own origins.** This applies to all of them, not just Cronus.
- Scholars study their origins constantly. Gods do not answer these questions.

---

## Planar Travel

- The planes are not sealed off — they are traversable.
- Any god, and any sufficiently powerful mortal, could travel to the Prelife or Postlife.
- The 13 Bound Gods have largely been to the Postlife (many were banished there at some point).
- This means: the Postlife is not a mystery to the gods. What is unknown is what it is like to *die* and cross as a living being — which only Tani has experienced (see Tani notes below).

---

## Tani — The Secret

- Tani was killed during the Week of Crimson Rain. She died, crossed over, and came back as a god.
- **Every god knows what the Postlife is** — they can visit, and many have been banished there. This is not her secret.
- Her secret is what happened **between the moment of her death and the moment she woke as a god** — a gap that no other being has experienced from the living side.
- The other twelve are aware there is something she is not saying. She does not deny it.

---

## Cronus — The Secret

- **Was mortal.** The only one of the thirteen who began as a living man.
- He ended the Week of Crimson Rain not through divine power but through persuasion — proposed the Gods' Law as a framework, and those who accepted it stayed.
- In accepting the compact, the thirteen made him something they could not undo: one of them.
- **The twelve know.** They were there. The secret holds because none of them have chosen to break it.
- His clergy, worshippers, and the scholarly world do not know. Two research efforts have come close; both were quietly buried.

---

## Neutral Ground

- **Frae City** (Cronus's domain) is neutral ground — but it is not the only neutral ground on Talan.
- **Malagarra** (on the island of Basamortua in Midarra) is explicitly neutral by centuries of treaty — claimed by no kingdom, depended on by every navy.
- Other neutral territories likely exist. Do not describe Frae City as uniquely neutral.

---

## The Gods' Law — Key Constraints

- No direct governance of mortal populations.
- No open warfare between bound gods on the Material Plane.
- No unchecked divine control of mortal lives.
- Enforced by the Ethereal Plane itself (the veil between Layer 1 and Layer 2).
- The gods can still advise, inspire, grant divine power to priests, maintain domains, and play political games — within these constraints.

---

## The Corrupted God

- Not one of the 13. Not bound by the Gods' Law. Something older, something wrong.
- Breached from deep underground in 1321 MR when Golden Empire miners dug too deep.
- Actively defeated in 2135 MR by the Adventurers Guild — not destroyed, but wounded severely enough that its active influence collapsed.
- Its spawn still emerge from deep places. Some Guild members believe it is healing.

---

## Calendar

- **GR** (Gods Reign) — counts backward, used for pre–Week of Crimson Rain dates.
- **MR** (Mortal Reign) — counts forward, used for post–Week of Crimson Rain dates.
- Current year: **2532 MR**.
- The Week of Crimson Rain = 0 GR / 0 MR — the hinge point of all history.
